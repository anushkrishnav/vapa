Step 1: Unbox and Identify Components

What to do

  • Carefully unbox the telescope.
  • Ensure all components are present.

What not to do

  • Don’t handle delicate parts roughly.
  • Avoid discarding any packaging too soon.

Step 2: Assemble the Tripod

What to do

  • Set up the tripod on a stable surface.
  • Ensure it is level.

What not to do

  • Don’t place the tripod on uneven ground.
  • Avoid over-tightening screws.

Step 3: Attach the Telescope

What to do

  • Secure the telescope to the tripod.
  • Ensure all locks are tight.

What not to do

  • Don’t let the telescope remain unbalanced.
  • Avoid using excessive force on mounts.

Step 4: Align the Finder Scope

What to do

  • Point at a distant object for alignment.
  • Adjust the finder scope accordingly.

What not to do

  • Don’t skip calibration steps.
  • Avoid misaligning the finder scope.

Step 5: Test and Observe

What to do

  • Use a low-power eyepiece to test.
  • Adjust the focus for clarity.

What not to do

  • Don’t skip focusing adjustments.
  • Avoid observing during bad weather.
