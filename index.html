Step 1: Understand the Telescope

Familiarize yourself with the telescope's parts, including the tripod, mount, and eyepieces.

What not to do:

  • Do not handle the lenses without cleaning your hands.
  • Avoid forcing parts together; refer to the manual if unsure.
  • Do not expose the telescope to direct sunlight without a solar filter.
2
Step 2: Choose the Right Location

Select a dark, open area free from city lights to set up your telescope.

What not to do:

  • Do not set up near bright lights or reflective surfaces.
  • Avoid uneven or unstable ground to prevent tipping.
  • Do not place the telescope in high-traffic areas.
3
Step 3: Assemble the Tripod

Set up the tripod on stable ground and ensure it is level.

What not to do:

  • Do not tighten screws excessively to avoid damage.
  • Avoid placing the tripod on slippery surfaces.
  • Do not ignore alignment tools provided in the kit.
